## Welcome to Bethlemitas

This guide will walk you through accessing the platform, understanding your dashboard, and completing your first meaningful action based on your role. Whether you're a coordinator, teacher, or psycho-orientator, you'll be productive within minutes.

<Note>
**First Time Here?** Contact your institution's coordinator to receive your login credentials. All accounts are created by coordinators with the appropriate role and permissions.
</Note>

## Step 1: Access the Platform

### Login to Your Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Login Page">
    Open your web browser and go to your institution's Bethlemitas URL. You'll see the login page with the Bethlemitas branding.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Your Credentials">
    Enter your **document number** (número de documento) and the **password** provided by your coordinator.
    
    ```text
    Document Number: Your national ID or document number
    Password: Initial password from coordinator
    ```
    
    <Warning>
    If you see the error "Tu cuenta se encuentra suspendida" (Your account is suspended), contact your coordinator. Only active accounts (`id_state = 1`) can access the system.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access Your Dashboard">
    After successful authentication, you'll be redirected to your personalized dashboard at `/dashboard`.
    
    The authentication is handled by `AuthController::authenticate` (app/Http/Controllers/auth/AuthController.php:27).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Forgot Your Password?

If you've forgotten your password, you can reset it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Forgot Password' Link">
    On the login page, click the **Olvidé mi contraseña** (Forgot Password) link.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Your Email">
    Provide the email address associated with your account. The system will send a password reset link to this email.
    
    ```text
    Route: /forgot-password
    Controller: AuthController::sendResetLink
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Your Email">
    Look for an email with the subject line about password reset. Click the link in the email.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set New Password">
    Enter your new password (minimum 6 characters) and confirm it. You'll be redirected to the login page with a success message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3: Complete Your First Task

Now let's complete a meaningful action based on your role. Follow the guide for your specific role:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For Teachers: Create Your First Referral">
    Let's walk through creating a student referral when you identify a student who needs psycho-orientation support.
    
    ### Creating a Student Referral
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to Create Referral">
        From your dashboard, click **Create Referral** or navigate to `/create/referral`.
        
        You'll see a form to submit student information and referral details.
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Enter Student Information">
        Fill in the student's basic information:
        
        ```yaml
        Document Number: Student's national ID (required, 1-20 digits)
        Name: Student's first name (required)
        Last Name: Student's last name (required)
        Age: Student's age (optional, must be positive integer)
        Degree: Select the student's grade level (required)
        Group: Select the student's class group (required)
        ```
        
        <Note>
        The system automatically checks if the student already exists. If they're already in Active, PIAR, or DUA status, you'll receive an informational message and the referral won't be created.
        </Note>
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Provide Referral Details">
        Document why you're referring this student:
        
        **Reason for Referral** (`reason_referral`):
        - Clear description of the behavioral, academic, or emotional concerns
        - Specific incidents or patterns you've observed
        
        **Observations** (`observation`):
        - Detailed observations about the student's behavior or performance
        - Contextual information about when and how issues manifest
        
        **Strategies Attempted** (`strategies`):
        - List interventions you've already tried in the classroom
        - Explain what worked and what didn't
        
        <Tip>
        Be as specific as possible. Quality referrals help psycho-orientators provide better support.
        </Tip>
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Submit the Referral">
        Click the submit button. The system will:
        
        1. **Validate** all required fields
        2. **Create** the student record in the database (if new)
        3. **Assign** the referral to the appropriate psycho-orientator based on the student's grade
        4. **Send** an email notification to the assigned psycho-orientator
        5. **Confirm** success with a message: "Estudiante remitido exitosamente"
        
        ```php
        // Behind the scenes (CreateReferralController.php:90-126)
        DB::beginTransaction();
        $user = new Users_student([...]);
        $referral = new Referral([...]);
        Mail::to($psico_date->email)->queue(new CreatedReferralMail());
        DB::commit();
        ```
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Monitor Your Referral">
        Navigate to **My Referrals** (`/index/students/remitted`) to see:
        - List of all students you've referred
        - Current status (Active, PIAR, or DUA)
        - Ability to edit student information if needed
        
        You can search by student name, last name, or document number.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    ### What Happens Next?
    
    After you submit a referral:
    
    1. The assigned psycho-orientator receives an email notification
    2. They review your referral details in their dashboard
    3. They may update the student information or referral if needed
    4. They create a psychological report with recommendations
    5. They may change the student's state to PIAR or DUA if specialized intervention is needed
    6. You can track the student's status in your **My Referrals** list
    
    <Card title="Next: Editing Student Information" icon="pen" href="/features/student-referrals">
      Learn more about managing referrals and editing student information
    </Card>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="For Psycho-Orientators: Review Your First Referral">
    Let's walk through reviewing a referral and creating a psychological report.
    
    ### Reviewing Pending Referrals
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access Pending Referrals">
        From your dashboard, click **Pending Referrals** or navigate to `/index/students/remitted/psico`.
        
        You'll see a list of students with "activo" status in the grade levels assigned to you.
        
        <Note>
        You only see students from grades you're assigned to via the `users_load_degrees` table. Coordinators configure these assignments.
        </Note>
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Review Referral Details">
        Click on a student to view their referral details (`/details/referral/{id}`).
        
        You'll see:
        - **Student Information**: Name, document number, age, degree, group
        - **Referring Teacher**: Who submitted the referral
        - **Referral Date**: When the referral was created
        - **Reason**: Why the student was referred
        - **Observations**: Teacher's detailed observations
        - **Strategies Tried**: Interventions already attempted
        
        Review all information carefully to understand the student's needs.
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Update Referral if Needed">
        If you need to correct or update any information, you can edit:
        - Student demographics (name, document, age, degree, group)
        - Referral details (reason, observation, strategies)
        
        Changes are saved when you click **Update**.
        
        ```php
        // PsicoController::update_details_referral (app/Http/Controllers/PsicoController.php:157)
        $student->update([...]);
        $referral->update([...]);
        ```
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Create Psychological Report">
        Click **Create Report** or navigate to `/report/student/{id}`.
        
        Fill in the comprehensive report form:
        
        **Report Title** (`title_report`):
        - Descriptive title for this psychological orientation report
        - Example: "Initial Assessment - Behavioral Concerns"
        
        **Reason for Inquiry** (`reason_inquiry`):
        - Detailed analysis of why the student is being evaluated
        - Your professional assessment of the situation
        - Any additional observations from your interaction with the student
        
        **Recommendations** (`recomendations`):
        - Specific intervention strategies
        - Support mechanisms to implement
        - Follow-up actions required
        - Timeline for reassessment
        
        **Student State** (`state`):
        - **Active**: Student needs monitoring but no specialized plan yet
        - **PIAR**: Student requires Individualized Educational Adjustment Plan
        - **DUA**: Student follows Universal Learning Design principles
        
        **Supporting Documentation** (`annex_one`):
        - Optional: Upload PDF attachments (max 5MB)
        - Examples: Assessment results, external reports, consent forms
        - Files stored in `storage/app/public/annexes/student_{id}/`
        
        <Tip>
        Comprehensive reports lead to better interventions. Include specific, actionable recommendations.
        </Tip>
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Save the Report">
        Click **Save Report**. The system will:
        
        1. **Update** student information (if changed)
        2. **Change** student state (if you selected PIAR or DUA)
        3. **Store** uploaded PDF (if provided)
        4. **Create** psycho-orientation record in database
        5. **Redirect** to your pending referrals list with success message
        
        ```php
        // PsicoController::store_report_student (app/Http/Controllers/PsicoController.php:215)
        DB::beginTransaction();
        $student->update(['id_state' => $request->state]);
        Psychoorientation::create([...]);
        DB::commit();
        ```
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Monitor Student Progress">
        Access the student from the appropriate list:
        - **Active Students**: `/psico/students/active` (all students in your grades)
        - **PIAR Students**: `/psico/students/piar` (students with individualized plans)
        - **DUA Students**: `/psico/students/dua` (universal design students)
        
        Click any student to view their complete history.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    ### Viewing Student History
    
    To see a comprehensive timeline of a student's interventions:
    
    1. Navigate to any student list
    2. Click on a student
    3. Click **View History** or go to `/student/history/{id}`
    
    You'll see:
    - **All Referrals**: Every referral submitted for this student with dates
    - **All Reports**: Every psychological report you've created
    - **Chronological Timeline**: Complete audit trail of interventions
    
    Click individual referrals or reports to view full details and edit if needed.
    
    <Card title="Next: Managing PIAR Students" icon="clipboard-list" href="/features/psycho-orientation">
      Learn advanced workflows for managing students with specialized intervention plans
    </Card>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="For Coordinators: Create Your First User">
    Let's walk through creating a teacher account and assigning them to classes.
    
    ### Creating a Teacher Account
    
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to User Management">
        From your dashboard, click **Create User** or navigate to `/create/user`.
        
        You'll see the user creation form.
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Enter User Information">
        Fill in the teacher's details:
        
        ```yaml
        Name: Teacher's first name
        Last Name: Teacher's last name
        Email: Valid email address (for password resets)
        Document Number: National ID number
        Phone: Contact phone number
        Password: Initial password (teacher will change on first login)
        Role: Select "Docente" (Teacher) or "Psicoorientador" (Psycho-orientator)
        State: Set to "Activo" (Active) to allow immediate login
        ```
        
        <Warning>
        Users with state "Suspendido" (Suspended) cannot log in. Use this to temporarily disable accounts without deleting them.
        </Warning>
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Save the User">
        Click **Save**. The system creates the user account with the selected role using Spatie Permissions.
        
        ```php
        // CreateController::store_user
        $user = new Users_teacher([...]);
        $user->assignRole($request->role); // 'docente' or 'psicoorientador'
        $user->save();
        ```
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Assign Teacher to Classes (For Teachers)">
        If you created a teacher (not psycho-orientator), assign them to classes:
        
        1. Navigate to **Create Group** (`/create/group`)
        2. Select the teacher, degree, group, and subject area
        3. Click **Assign**
        
        This creates a record in `teachers_areas_groups` linking the teacher to specific classes.
        
        Example: Assign "María González" to teach Mathematics to Group 6A.
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Assign Psycho-orientator to Grades (For Psycho-orientators)">
        If you created a psycho-orientator, assign them to grade levels:
        
        1. The assignment interface (typically in grade management)
        2. Select the psycho-orientator and the grades they'll cover
        3. Save the assignment
        
        This creates records in `users_load_degrees` so referrals are automatically routed correctly.
        
        Example: Assign "Dr. Juan Pérez" to handle grades 6, 7, and 8.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    
    ### Managing Academic Structure
    
    Before assigning teachers, ensure your academic structure is configured:
    
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Create Degrees (Grade Levels)" icon="layer-group">
        Navigate to **Create Degree** (`/create/degree`):
        
        1. Enter degree name (e.g., "6", "7", "8", "9", "10", "11")
        2. Click **Save**
        3. Repeat for all grade levels in your institution
        
        Degrees are ordered numerically for display.
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Create Groups (Class Sections)" icon="users-rectangle">
        Navigate to **Create Group** (`/create/group`):
        
        1. Enter group name (e.g., "A", "B", "C")
        2. Click **Save**
        3. Repeat for all sections
        
        Groups combine with degrees (e.g., 6A, 6B, 7A, 7B).
      </Accordion>
      
      <Accordion title="Create Areas (Subject Areas)" icon="book">
        Navigate to **Create Area** (`/create/area`):
        
        1. Enter area name (e.g., "Mathematics", "Science", "Language Arts")
        2. Click **Save**
        3. Repeat for all subjects taught
        
        Areas are assigned to teachers for specific groups.
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
    
    ### View All Users
    
    Navigate to **User List** (`/listing/users`) to see:
    - All teachers and psycho-orientators
    - Current roles and status
    - Edit or suspend accounts as needed
    
    You can search by name, document number, or email.
    
    <Card title="Next: Advanced User Management" icon="users-gear" href="/features/user-management">
      Learn how to edit users, manage permissions, and configure complex assignments
    </Card>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Profile">
    Click **My Profile** (Mi Perfil) in the navigation menu or go to `/mi-perfil`.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Email">
    Change your email address for password reset and notifications.
    
    ```text
    Route: PUT /mi-perfil/email
    Controller: ProfileController::updateEmail
    ```

  <Step title="Update Profile Photo">


## Common Tasks Quick Reference

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Search for Students" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Most list views (referrals, student lists) include a search box. You can search by:
    - Student first name
    - Student last name
    - Document number
    - Full name (first + last)
    
    The search is case-insensitive and matches partial strings.
    
    ```php
    // Example: PsicoController search implementation (line 120-124)
    $query->where('name', 'LIKE', "%$search%")
          ->orWhere('last_name', 'LIKE', "%$search%")
          ->orWhere('number_documment', 'LIKE', "%$search%");
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Download PDF Attachments" icon="file-pdf">
    Psycho-orientators can attach PDFs to reports. To view attachments:
    
    1. Open student history
    2. Click on a report with an attachment
    3. Click the **Download** link
    
    Files are stored in `storage/app/public/annexes/student_{id}/` and served via Laravel's storage link.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Log Out Safely" icon="right-from-bracket">
    Always log out when finished, especially on shared computers:
    
    1. Click **Logout** in the navigation menu
    2. Or navigate directly to `/logout`
    
    This clears your session and prevents unauthorized access.
    
    ```php
    // AuthController::logout (app/Http/Controllers/auth/AuthController.php:19)
    Auth::logout();
    Session::flush();
    Session::regenerate(true);
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Handle Error Messages" icon="circle-exclamation">
    If you encounter error messages:
    
    - **"Tu cuenta se encuentra suspendida"**: Contact your coordinator to reactivate your account
    - **"El estudiante ya fue remitido"**: Student already has an active referral; check My Referrals list
    - **"No se encontró un psicoorientador asignado"**: Coordinator needs to assign a psycho-orientator to this grade level
    - **"Número de documento ó contraseña son incorrectas"**: Check your credentials and try again
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
